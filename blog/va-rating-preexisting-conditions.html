VA Disability for Pre-Existing Conditions: Can You Still Get Compensation? (2026)

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Many veterans assume that if they had a health condition before enlisting, they can't get VA disability for it. This is one of the most widespread misconceptions about the VA system — and it costs veterans significant compensation. Here's the truth about pre-existing conditions and VA disability.

⚡ The Critical Rule Most Veterans Don't Know

The VA must presume that every veteran entered service in sound condition — the "presumption of soundness." If you were cleared by your entry physical, the VA must assume you were healthy when you entered. The burden is then on the VA to show your condition existed before service AND was not aggravated by service. This presumption strongly protects veterans.

The Presumption of Soundness

Under 38 USC §1111, veterans are presumed to have been in sound condition when they entered service — except for conditions "noted" in their entry examination. If a condition was not recorded on your entry exam, the VA cannot use it as a pre-existing condition to deny your claim without clear and unmistakable evidence that it pre-existed service AND was not aggravated by service.

Aggravation — The Key Pathway for Pre-Existing Conditions

Even if a condition genuinely pre-existed service, it can still be service connected if military service permanently aggravated it beyond its natural progression. The standard requires showing:

  • The condition existed before service (documented or acknowledged)
  • Military service increased the severity of the condition, AND
  • The increase was permanent — not just a temporary flare-up

Example: A veteran with mild scoliosis before service who carried heavy equipment for 20 years and now has severe lumbar spine disease has a strong aggravation claim — military service clearly worsened what was a mild pre-existing condition.

Common Pre-Existing Conditions Veterans Successfully Claim

  • Knee conditions — Mild arthritis or injury pre-service worsened by military physical demands
  • Back conditions — Pre-service disc issues dramatically worsened by military service
  • Flat feet — Congenital condition aggravated by military marching (see our flat feet guide)
  • Mental health conditions — Pre-existing anxiety or depression dramatically worsened by combat or service trauma
  • Hearing — Mild pre-existing hearing loss substantially worsened by military noise exposure

How to Document Aggravation

  • Get a nexus letter from your doctor stating the military service "aggravated" the condition beyond natural progression. See our nexus letter guide.
  • Obtain pre-service medical records if available — but note that absence of pre-service records actually helps you under the presumption of soundness rule
  • Document the specific military duties that worsened the condition (occupational specialty, physical demands, documented injuries)
  • Get current medical evidence showing the severity of the condition now
