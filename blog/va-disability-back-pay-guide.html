VA Disability Back Pay: How Much Can You Get? (2026)

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

VA disability back pay — also called retroactive compensation — is the lump sum payment covering the period from your effective date to when your rating was approved. For many veterans, this is thousands or tens of thousands of dollars. Here's exactly how it works and how to maximize it.

💰 Back Pay Fast Facts

Your effective date determines how far back your back pay goes. The earliest possible effective date is typically the day you filed your claim. Some situations allow for an earlier effective date — including the Intent to File rule, rating increases, and specific circumstances. How Effective Dates Work

Your effective date is the date from which your VA compensation is calculated. Everything from the effective date to your approval date is owed to you as a lump sum back payment. The standard rule:

  • The effective date is the date your claim was received by the VA, or the date you were first entitled to benefits — whichever is later
  • If you filed within one year of discharge from service, your effective date can be the day after discharge

The Intent to File — Protecting Your Effective Date

Filing an Intent to File (VA Form 21-0966) establishes your effective date up to one year before you submit your complete claim. This gives you 12 months to gather evidence and file the full claim while protecting your start date. This is one of the most valuable and underused tools in the VA system.

Example: You file an Intent to File on January 1, 2026. You gather evidence and submit your complete claim on September 1, 2026. Your effective date is January 1, 2026 — not September 1. You receive 8 months of back pay you would have lost without the Intent to File.

How to Calculate Your Back Pay

Your back pay = (your monthly compensation rate) × (number of months from effective date to approval date)

Example: Approved at 70% ($1,760.58/month) with an effective date 18 months ago = $1,760.58 × 18 = $31,690.44 lump sum

Situations That Allow Earlier Effective Dates

  • Within one year of discharge — Effective date can be day after discharge
  • Rating increases — If your condition worsened and you file for an increase, the effective date is when you filed for the increase (or when the worsening became documented)
  • Clear and Unmistakable Error (CUE) — If the VA made an undeniable error in a past decision, you can request correction back to the original date
  • Reopened claims — Supplemental claims can establish earlier effective dates in some situations
  • Direct service connection established after secondary — Complex situations requiring VSO or attorney guidance

How Back Pay Is Paid

Back pay is paid as a single lump sum directly deposited into your account, typically within 15–30 days of your final rating decision. It arrives separately from your first regular monthly payment.
