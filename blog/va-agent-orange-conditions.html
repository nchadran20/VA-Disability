VA Disability for Agent Orange: Full Presumptive List 2026

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Agent Orange was a herbicide used extensively during the Vietnam War and in other locations. The VA has established a list of conditions presumptively service connected for veterans exposed to Agent Orange — meaning no proof of exposure is required, only proof of service in a covered location and a current diagnosis. Here's the complete 2026 list.

🎖️ Agent Orange Fast Facts

If you served in Vietnam, the Korean DMZ, certain Thailand bases, or other covered locations during covered periods, you are presumed to have been exposed to Agent Orange. With exposure presumed, you only need a current diagnosis of any qualifying condition to receive service connection — no proof linking the condition to service required.

Who Has Presumed Agent Orange Exposure

  • Vietnam veterans — Served in the Republic of Vietnam between January 9, 1962 and May 7, 1975 (including offshore waters)
  • Korean DMZ veterans — Served in the Korean DMZ between September 1, 1967 and August 31, 1971
  • Thailand veterans — Served at certain Royal Thai Air Force Bases between February 28, 1961 and May 7, 1975
  • C-123 aircraft veterans — Served on C-123 aircraft that were used for Agent Orange spraying
  • Johnston Island veterans — Served at Johnston Island where Agent Orange was stored
  • Blue Water Navy — Served on vessels in the territorial seas of Vietnam (expanded coverage)

How to File an Agent Orange Claim

  1. Gather your service records confirming service in a covered location during covered dates
  2. Get medical documentation of your current qualifying condition
  3. File VA Form 21-526EZ — list each condition as an Agent Orange presumptive
  4. You do NOT need a nexus letter for presumptive conditions — the exposure is presumed from your service records

Diabetes Type 2 — The Most Common Agent Orange Claim

Type 2 diabetes is one of the most common Agent Orange presumptive claims and one of the most valuable due to its many secondary conditions. A veteran with service-connected diabetes can also claim diabetic neuropathy, retinopathy, nephropathy, and erectile dysfunction as secondary conditions — potentially adding hundreds of dollars per month to their combined rating.
