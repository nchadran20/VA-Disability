How to Get a 100% VA Rating: Schedular vs TDIU (2026 Guide)

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

A 100% VA disability rating is the highest possible rating — worth $3,737.85/month tax-free for a veteran alone in 2026, more with dependents. There are two paths to 100%: schedular (your combined rating genuinely reaches 100%) and TDIU (total disability based on individual unemployability). Here's the honest guide to both.

📊 100% Rating Fast Facts

100% Schedular: $3,737.85/month alone in 2026. 100% with spouse: $3,946.25/month. TDIU pays the same as 100% even if your combined rating is lower. Veterans at 100% also get Priority Group 1 healthcare, free VA healthcare, and many state benefits. Use our pay calculator for your exact amount.

Path 1: Schedular 100% — Reaching 100% Through Ratings

Due to VA math (combined ratings, not simple addition), reaching a schedular 100% requires a combination of conditions that together create a "whole person" rating of 100%. Here are common rating combinations that reach 100%:

Example CombinationCombined Rating Result
70% PTSD + 50% Sleep Apnea + 30% Back + 10% Tinnitus~93% → rounded to 90%... need more
70% PTSD + 50% Sleep Apnea + 50% Back + 30% Knee + 10% Tinnitus~97% → rounded to 100%
100% single condition (PTSD, TBI, or other total impairment)100% directly

Path 2: TDIU — The Faster Route for Many Veterans

Total Disability based on Individual Unemployability pays you at the 100% rate even if your combined rating is lower — as long as your service-connected conditions prevent you from maintaining substantially gainful employment. See our detailed TDIU guide for full qualification criteria and how to apply.

TDIU eligibility requires:

  • One condition rated at 60%+ (or multiple conditions with combined rating of 70%+ and one rated at least 40%), AND
  • Unable to maintain substantially gainful employment due to service-connected conditions

What 100% Rating Gets You Beyond the Monthly Pay

  • VA healthcare Priority Group 1 — No copays for any VA healthcare
  • Dental care — Full VA dental coverage at 100%
  • Dependents Educational Assistance (DEA) — Education benefits for your dependents
  • Vocational rehabilitation — Enhanced access to job training programs
  • State benefits — Most states have significant property tax exemptions, vehicle registration discounts, and other benefits for 100% rated veterans
  • Commissary and exchange access — Shopping at military stores

Common Conditions That Reach 100% Alone

  • PTSD rated at 100% (total social and occupational impairment)
  • TBI with total cognitive and social impairment
  • Cancer actively being treated
  • Severe cardiovascular conditions
  • Total blindness or deafness
