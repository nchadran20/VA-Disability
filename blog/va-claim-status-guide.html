VA Disability Claim Status: What Each Status Means (2026)

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Checking your VA claim status and seeing cryptic terms like "Preparation for Decision" or "Pending Decision Approval" can be confusing and stressful. Here's exactly what each status means, how long each stage typically takes, and what you should do at each phase.

📊 Where to Check Your Status

Check your claim status at va.gov/claim-or-appeal-status or through the VA mobile app. You'll need your va.gov login. Call 1-800-827-1000 to speak with a representative. Visit your local VA regional office in person for more detailed information.

VA Claim Status Stages Explained

StatusWhat It MeansTypical Duration
Claim ReceivedVA has your claim and is assigning it to a processor1–2 weeks
Initial ReviewVA is reviewing your claim for completeness and requesting records2–4 weeks
Evidence Gathering, Review, and DecisionVA is collecting service records, scheduling C&P exam, reviewing medical evidence3–12 months (most time spent here)
Preparation for DecisionA VA rater is actively working on your rating decision1–4 weeks
Pending Decision ApprovalRating is complete and being reviewed by a senior rater1–2 weeks
Preparation for NotificationDecision is final, rating letter is being prepared to mail1–2 weeks
CompleteDecision letter has been mailed — check your mail and va.gov documentsLetter arrives 1–2 weeks after this status

The Evidence Gathering Stage — Why It Takes So Long

Most of the total claim processing time is spent in the "Evidence Gathering, Review, and Decision" stage. During this phase the VA is:

  • Requesting your service treatment records (STRs) from military archives
  • Requesting records from VA healthcare facilities
  • Scheduling and completing your C&P exam (see our C&P exam guide)
  • Reviewing all evidence and drafting the rating decision

This stage varies from 3 months for simple claims to 18+ months for complex claims with multiple conditions.

What to Do While Waiting

  • Continue treating all claimed conditions — gaps in treatment can hurt your rating
  • Submit any additional evidence as soon as you have it — don't wait for the VA to ask
  • Attend your C&P exam — missing it can result in a denial
  • If your claim has been in Evidence Gathering for 6+ months with no C&P exam scheduled, call 1-800-827-1000 to inquire

"Preparation for Decision" — What It Really Means

When your status moves to "Preparation for Decision," it means a VA rater has your file and is actively writing your rating decision. This is generally good news — your claim is no longer waiting in a queue. Most claims move from "Preparation for Decision" to "Complete" within 2–6 weeks.

If Your Status Is Stuck

  • Claims stuck for 4+ months without status changes — contact your VSO or congressional representative's office for a congressional inquiry
  • A congressional inquiry often breaks a stuck claim loose within 2–4 weeks
  • Your local VSO can also contact the VA regional office on your behalf
