Why We Built This

The VA disability system is complicated — and that complexity costs veterans real money. Between VA math, secondary conditions, TDIU, Special Monthly Compensation, and the appeals process, most veterans are left piecing together information from outdated PDFs, Reddit threads, and YouTube videos of questionable accuracy.

Our founders are veterans and military family members who spent years navigating the VA system personally. We know firsthand what it's like to receive a rating that doesn't match your actual level of impairment, to not know what secondary conditions you could have claimed, or to miss the window on a supplemental claim because nobody told you the deadline was one year. This site exists to fix that information gap.

Our Core Principle

Every piece of content on this site is written to help veterans get the benefits they've earned — not to generate ad clicks or serve any commercial interest. If the information doesn't genuinely help a veteran, it doesn't belong here.

Our Review Process
Accuracy & Verification

Every article on this site goes through a three-step process: (1) research using primary sources — VA.gov, 38 CFR, VBA publications; (2) verification of all pay rates and rating criteria against current official tables; (3) review for accuracy and completeness before publication. We update articles when VA regulations, pay rates, or rating criteria change.

2026 Rate Updates
Data Currency

All pay rates on this site reflect the official 2026 VA compensation tables, updated for the 2.8% COLA adjustment effective December 1, 2025. We update our data annually when the VA publishes new rates and whenever significant regulatory changes occur that affect veterans' claims.

How We Research Our Content

All content on VA Disability Hub is sourced from official government publications and primary legal sources. We never publish second-hand information without verifying it against the primary source. Our primary references include:

Primary Sources We Use

  • VA.gov — Official VA disability compensation rates, eligibility criteria, and claims process guides
  • 38 Code of Federal Regulations (CFR) Parts 3 and 4 — The actual legal standards for rating disabilities, including all Diagnostic Codes
  • VBA Annual Benefits Reports — Official data on claim volumes, conditions, and processing times
  • Board of Veterans Appeals (BVA) decisions — Published decisions that establish how specific conditions and situations are adjudicated
  • Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims (CAVC) decisions — Appellate decisions that set legal standards for VA adjudication
  • VA Office of Inspector General (OIG) reports — Independent oversight findings on claims processing and benefit accuracy
