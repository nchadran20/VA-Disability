About VA Disability Hub

Independent · Free · Veteran-Focused

VA Disability Hub was built with one goal: give veterans a clear, comprehensive, and completely free resource to understand their disability ratings, monthly compensation, and the full scope of benefits they've earned through their service.

Why We Built This

The VA disability system is complicated by design — or at least it feels that way. Between VA math, secondary conditions, TDIU, Special Monthly Compensation, and the appeal process, most veterans are left piecing together information from outdated PDFs, Reddit threads, and YouTube videos.

We believed there should be one place where a veteran could come and get straight answers. Not a law firm trying to sign you up, not a government site buried in bureaucratic language — just clear, accurate information with real tools that actually help.

What We Offer

  • 2026 Pay Calculator — enter your rating and dependents, get your exact monthly and annual pay
  • Complete Pay Tables — official 2026 rates for every rating level and dependency status
  • Condition Database — typical ratings for 24+ common service-connected conditions
  • Combined Rating Calculator — uses real VA math to show your actual combined rating
  • Benefits Guide — what each rating level unlocks beyond monthly pay
  • TDIU Guide — the most overlooked benefit in the VA system
  • Claim Checklist — interactive step-by-step filing guide
  • In-depth Articles — covering specific conditions, secondary claims, and appeals

Our Commitment to Accuracy

All pay rates on this site reflect official VA.gov figures updated for the 2026 COLA adjustment (2.8%, effective December 1, 2025). We update our data as official rates change each year. Our articles are researched using primary sources including VA.gov, 38 CFR regulations, and published VA research.

We Are Not a Law Firm

VA Disability Hub is an independent informational resource. We are not affiliated with the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs, and nothing on this site constitutes legal advice. For personalized claims assistance, we strongly recommend connecting with:

  • VSOs (Veterans Service Organizations) — DAV, VFW, American Legion, and others provide free, accredited claims assistance
  • VA-Accredited Claims Agents — licensed to represent veterans before the VA
  • VA-Accredited Attorneys — especially valuable for appeals to the Board of Veterans Appeals
