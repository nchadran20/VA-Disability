How to File a VA Disability Claim: Step-by-Step Guide (2026)

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Filing a VA disability claim for the first time can feel overwhelming — but the process is more straightforward than most veterans expect. Here's the complete step-by-step guide for 2026, from gathering evidence to submitting your claim and understanding what happens next.

📋 Filing Fast Facts

The primary form is VA Form 21-526EZ — Application for Disability Compensation. You can file online at va.gov, by mail, or in person at a VA regional office. Filing is free. VSOs can help you file at no cost. Average processing time in 2026: 3–6 months for initial claims.

Step 1: Determine What to Claim

Make a list of every health condition you believe was caused or worsened by your military service. Don't self-filter — if you think there might be a connection, include it. You can always add more conditions later, but your effective date (and back pay) starts from the date you file. Review our secondary conditions guide to identify conditions you might not have considered.

Step 2: Gather Your Evidence

Strong claims have three elements:

  • Service nexus — Evidence that the condition is connected to service (service records, in-service medical records, buddy statements)
  • Current diagnosis — Medical records showing you currently have the condition
  • Severity documentation — Records showing how badly the condition affects you

For secondary conditions, you also need a nexus letter from your doctor connecting the secondary condition to your primary service-connected condition.

Step 3: File VA Form 21-526EZ

The easiest way to file in 2026 is online through va.gov/disability/apply. You'll need:

  • Your Social Security number and VA file number
  • Information about your military service (dates, branch, discharge type)
  • A list of all conditions you're claiming
  • Medical evidence files to upload (PDFs work best)

Alternatively, you can work with a VSO (Veterans Service Organization) who will help you complete and file the form at no cost.

Step 4: Attend Your C&P Exam

After filing, the VA will schedule a Compensation and Pension exam to assess your conditions. This is the most important appointment in your claim. See our complete C&P exam guide for exactly how to prepare and what mistakes to avoid.

Step 5: Receive Your Rating Decision

After your C&P exam, the VA issues a Rating Decision letter — typically 3–6 months after filing. Review it carefully for:

  • Which conditions were service connected and which were denied
  • The rating percentage assigned to each condition
  • The effective date (this determines back pay)
  • Your right to appeal within one year

If You're Denied or Under-Rated

A denial or low rating is not the end — it's the beginning of the appeal process. You have three options within one year: Supplemental Claim (new evidence), Higher-Level Review (error in decision), or Board of Veterans Appeals. See our appeal guide for the full breakdown of each option.

Free Help Filing Your Claim

  • DAV (Disabled American Veterans) — Free claim filing assistance nationwide
  • VFW — Veterans of Foreign Wars service officers
  • American Legion — Free VSO services
  • State VA offices — Your state's Department of Veterans Affairs

Never pay a company to file a VA claim — VSOs provide this service completely free.
