VA Rating for Flat Feet (Pes Planus): 2026 Complete Guide

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Flat feet — pes planus — is one of the top 20 most claimed VA conditions and extremely common among veterans whose service involved marching, heavy loads, and prolonged standing. Here's exactly how the VA rates it in 2026.

👟 Flat Feet Fast Facts

Rated under Diagnostic Code 5276. Ratings range from 0–50% per foot. Secondary conditions like plantar fasciitis, knee problems, and back pain are routinely claimed alongside flat feet. Use our combined rating calculator to see your total.

VA Flat Feet Rating Table (DC 5276)

Acquired vs Congenital — Why It Matters

Acquired flat feet are easier to service connect — you show the condition developed or worsened during service. Congenital flat feet existed before service but can still be service connected if service permanently aggravated them beyond natural progression.

Secondary Claims — The Real Value of Flat Feet

  • Plantar fasciitis — Flat feet directly cause plantar fascia inflammation. See our plantar fasciitis guide.
  • Knee conditions — Altered gait stresses knees. See our knee conditions guide.
  • Back pain — Gait changes cause compensatory lumbar strain. See our back pain guide.
  • Hip conditions — Same biomechanical chain — flat feet to hip stress.
