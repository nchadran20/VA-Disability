VA Disability for Gulf War Syndrome: 2026 Presumptive List and Guide

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Gulf War Syndrome refers to a cluster of chronic, unexplained symptoms affecting Gulf War veterans — and the VA has specific rules that make it easier to service connect these conditions than typical claims. Here's the complete 2026 guide to what qualifies, who is eligible, and how to file.

🎖️ Gulf War Fast Facts

Gulf War presumptive coverage applies to veterans who served in Southwest Asia from August 2, 1990 to the present — including Operation Desert Storm, Iraqi Freedom, and Enduring Freedom. You do NOT need to prove your condition was caused by a specific exposure — just that you served in the region and have a qualifying condition.

Who Qualifies for Gulf War Presumptives

Veterans who served in the following areas since August 2, 1990:

  • Iraq, Kuwait, Saudi Arabia
  • The neutral zone between Iraq and Saudi Arabia
  • Bahrain, Qatar, United Arab Emirates, Oman
  • Afghanistan (post-9/11 coverage under PACT Act)
  • Waters in the Persian Gulf, Red Sea, Arabian Sea, Gulf of Oman, Gulf of Aden
  • Airspace above these areas

Undiagnosed Illness Claims — A Unique Gulf War Benefit

Gulf War veterans can receive disability compensation for undiagnosed illnesses — conditions that don't fit neatly into a standard diagnosis but cause real symptoms. This is unique to Gulf War veterans. You must show:

  • The condition began during or after active duty in the Southwest Asia theater
  • Symptoms have persisted for 6 months or more
  • The condition is chronic and disabling to a degree of at least 10%

PACT Act Expansion for Gulf War Veterans

The 2022 PACT Act expanded benefits for toxic-exposed veterans including Gulf War veterans. Additional conditions related to burn pit exposure, airborne hazards, and contaminated water are now covered. See our PACT Act guide for the full list of new presumptives.

How to File a Gulf War Claim

  1. Gather your deployment records confirming service in Southwest Asia
  2. Get medical documentation of your current conditions and when symptoms began
  3. File VA Form 21-526EZ listing each condition
  4. Specify Gulf War presumptive on the form — the VA should apply presumptive service connection automatically based on your deployment history
