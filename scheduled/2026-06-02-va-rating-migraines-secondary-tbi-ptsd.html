VA Rating for Migraines Secondary to TBI or PTSD: 2026 Guide

Updated: June 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Migraines as a secondary condition to TBI or PTSD is one of the most scientifically supported and legally achievable secondary VA claims. If you have service-connected TBI or PTSD and suffer from migraines, you are almost certainly entitled to a separate migraine rating on top of your existing rating. Here's exactly how to claim it.

🧠 Secondary Migraine Fast Facts

Migraines are rated under Diagnostic Code 8100. Ratings are 0%, 10%, 30%, or 50% based on frequency and prostrating attacks. A 50% migraine rating (very frequent prostrating attacks) pays $1,075.16/month on top of your existing rating. Secondary to TBI is extremely well-supported scientifically. Use our combined rating calculator to see how this affects your total.

Why TBI and PTSD Cause Migraines

  • TBI — Traumatic brain injury disrupts normal brain function and neurochemistry. Post-traumatic headaches and migraines affect up to 90% of TBI patients and are the most common TBI secondary condition. The connection is so well-established that most VA examiners readily acknowledge it.
  • PTSD — PTSD causes hypervigilance, chronic stress, and neurological dysregulation — all of which trigger migraine pathways. Veterans with PTSD have significantly higher rates of migraine than the general population.

How the VA Rates Migraines (DC 8100)

RatingFrequency of Prostrating AttacksMonthly Pay (Added, Alone)
0%Less than once every 2 months, little economic inadaptability$0 (establishes service connection)
10%Characteristic prostrating attacks, average once every 2 months over last several months+$175.51
30%Characteristic prostrating attacks occurring on average once per month over last several months+$524.31
50%Very frequent completely prostrating and prolonged attacks productive of severe economic inadaptability+$1,075.16

What "Prostrating" Means

A prostrating migraine attack is one that forces you to stop what you're doing and rest or lie down — you cannot continue working or your normal activities. Document this specifically: "My migraines cause me to stop work/activities and lie down in a dark room for X hours." This language directly maps to the rating criteria.

How to Establish the Secondary Connection

  1. Get a current migraine diagnosis documented by your doctor
  2. Have your doctor write a nexus letter stating it is "at least as likely as not" that your migraines are caused by or worsened by your service-connected TBI or PTSD. See our nexus letter guide.
  3. File VA Form 21-526EZ listing migraines as secondary to TBI or PTSD
  4. Submit a personal statement documenting migraine frequency, duration, and how they affect your ability to work

This is distinct from our general migraine guide which covers direct service connection — this article specifically addresses the secondary connection pathway through TBI or PTSD.
