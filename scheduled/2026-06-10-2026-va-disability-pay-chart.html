2026 VA Disability Pay Chart: Complete Rate Table with Dependents

Updated: June 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

The 2026 VA disability compensation rates include a 2.8% Cost-of-Living Adjustment (COLA) effective December 1, 2025. These are the current rates paid to veterans with service-connected disabilities as of January 2026. Here are the complete pay tables for every rating level and dependent combination.

📊 2026 COLA Update

VA disability pay increased 2.8% in 2026 — the same COLA applied to Social Security benefits. The increase took effect December 1, 2025 and veterans received their first 2026-rate payment in January 2026. No action was required — the increase was applied automatically. Special Monthly Compensation (SMC) — Additional Pay Above 100%

Veterans with severe disabilities may receive Special Monthly Compensation (SMC) on top of their regular rating pay. The most common SMC levels:

  • SMC-K (erectile dysfunction, loss of creative organ): +$126.77/month
  • SMC-S (housebound): +$367.30/month added to existing pay

See our complete SMC guide for all levels and how to qualify.

What Is VA Disability Pay Based On?

Your monthly payment depends on three factors: your combined disability rating (calculated using VA math — see our combined ratings guide), your number of dependents (spouse, children, dependent parents), and whether you qualify for SMC. All VA disability compensation is completely tax-free at the federal and state level.
