How Long Does a VA Disability Claim Take in 2026?

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

One of the most common questions veterans ask is how long the VA disability claims process takes. The honest answer: it varies — but here are the real average processing times for 2026 and what you can do to avoid delays.

⏱️ 2026 Processing Time Summary

Initial claims average: 3–6 months. Supplemental Claims: 4–5 months. Higher-Level Reviews: 4–5 months. Board of Veterans Appeals (Direct Review): 1–2 years. Board of Veterans Appeals (Hearing): 3–5+ years. These are averages — individual claims can be faster or slower.

Average Processing Times by Claim Type (2026)

Claim TypeAverage Processing TimeNotes
Initial Disability Claim3–6 monthsSimple 1–2 condition claims faster; complex multi-condition claims longer
Supplemental Claim4–5 monthsRequires new and relevant evidence
Higher-Level Review4–5 monthsUses existing evidence; senior rater review
BVA — Direct Review1–2 yearsNo hearing; judge reviews existing record
BVA — Evidence Submission1–3 yearsSubmit new evidence without hearing
BVA — Hearing Request3–5+ yearsFormal hearing before Veterans Law Judge

What Causes Delays

  • Missing records — The VA requesting service treatment records from the National Personnel Records Center can take months
  • C&P exam scheduling — High demand for examiners, especially in certain regions
  • Multiple conditions — More conditions = more records to gather, more C&P exams to schedule
  • Complex evidence — Claims requiring specialist examiners or extensive private medical records take longer
  • Regional office backlog — Some VA regional offices have much longer average times than others

How to Speed Up Your Claim

  • Submit all evidence upfront — Don't wait for the VA to request it. Upload everything when you file.
  • Get a nexus letter before filing — Having private medical opinions ready eliminates months of back-and-forth. See our nexus letter guide.
  • Respond to VA requests immediately — Any VA request for information starts a 30-day clock. Respond the same day if possible.
  • Don't miss your C&P exam — Missing it causes delays of months and may result in denial. See our C&P exam guide.
  • Use a VSO — VSOs know how to package claims correctly to minimize back-and-forth

The Fully Developed Claim Program

Filing a Fully Developed Claim (FDC) — where you certify that all evidence has been submitted and you're not waiting for any additional records — can speed up processing at some regional offices. However, FDC has become less important since the RAMP and AMA modernization, and most claims are now processed similarly regardless of FDC designation.
