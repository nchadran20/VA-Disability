VA Rating for Ankle and Foot Conditions: 2026 Complete Guide

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Ankle and foot conditions are among the most commonly claimed musculoskeletal VA disabilities — unsurprising given the physical demands of military service. Here's exactly how the VA rates ankle conditions, common foot injuries, and the secondary claims they support.

👟 Ankle/Foot Fast Facts

The VA rates ankle conditions primarily under DC 5270–5274 based on range of motion and functional limitation. Flat feet and plantar fasciitis are rated under separate diagnostic codes. Multiple foot conditions can each receive separate ratings. Use our combined rating calculator.

Common Ankle and Foot Conditions for Veterans

  • Chronic ankle instability — Repeated sprains during service cause ligament laxity and chronic instability, rated under DC 5270–5271
  • Achilles tendon injury — Tendinitis, partial tears, and ruptures are rated based on functional limitation and pain
  • Metatarsal conditions — Stress fractures and metatarsalgia (pain in the ball of the foot) rated under DC 5279
  • Hallux valgus (bunions) — Rated under DC 5280, common in veterans with extensive marching history

Secondary Conditions to Claim

  • Flat feet (pes planus) — Ankle instability and altered mechanics contribute to flat feet. See our flat feet guide.
  • Plantar fasciitis — Ankle and foot mechanics directly cause plantar fascia stress. See our plantar fasciitis guide.
  • Knee conditions — Ankle problems alter gait and stress knees. See our knee guide.
  • Back conditions — Compensatory gait causes lumbar strain. See our back pain guide.
