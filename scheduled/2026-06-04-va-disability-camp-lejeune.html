VA Disability for Camp Lejeune Veterans: 2026 Complete Guide

Updated: June 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Veterans and family members who lived or worked at Camp Lejeune between 1953 and 1987 were exposed to contaminated drinking water containing toxic chemicals including benzene, trichloroethylene (TCE), and other volatile organic compounds. The PACT Act of 2022 significantly expanded VA coverage for these veterans. Here's exactly what you're entitled to in 2026.

🎖️ Camp Lejeune Fast Facts

Eligibility: Served at Camp Lejeune or MCAS New River for at least 30 days between August 1, 1953 and December 31, 1987. Presumptive conditions: 8 cancers and 7 non-cancer conditions — no proof of exposure needed beyond service records. Also applies to family members who lived on base during the covered period for healthcare (not disability compensation).

Presumptive Conditions for Camp Lejeune Veterans

CategorySpecific Conditions
Presumptive CancersBladder cancer, kidney cancer, liver cancer, non-Hodgkin's lymphoma, multiple myeloma, leukemia, cervical cancer, breast cancer (male and female)
Non-Cancer Presumptive ConditionsNeurobehavioral effects, renal toxicity (kidney damage), hepatic steatosis (fatty liver disease), female infertility, miscarriage, scleroderma, end-stage renal disease (ESRD)

Filing a Camp Lejeune Claim

  1. Verify your service dates — Gather any documents proving you served at Camp Lejeune or MCAS New River between August 1953 and December 1987 for at least 30 days
  2. Get current diagnosis documentation — Medical records confirming your qualifying condition
  3. File VA Form 21-526EZ — List your qualifying condition and note it as a Camp Lejeune presumptive condition
  4. No nexus letter required — The presumption handles service connection; you don't need to prove exposure caused the condition

Camp Lejeune Justice Act — Civil Claims Separate from VA

The Camp Lejeune Justice Act of 2022 also allows veterans and family members to file civil lawsuits against the federal government for Camp Lejeune contamination. These civil claims are entirely separate from VA disability benefits — you can pursue both simultaneously. The VA disability claim is no-cost and should be filed regardless of any civil claim status.

If Your Claim Was Previously Denied

Many Camp Lejeune claims were denied before the PACT Act expanded coverage in 2022. If your claim was previously denied, file a Supplemental Claim citing the new PACT Act presumptives as new and relevant evidence. The VA should reopen and approve many previously denied Camp Lejeune claims under current rules. See our appeal options guide for the process.
