VA Rating for Shin Splints and Stress Fractures: 2026 Guide

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Shin splints and stress fractures are extremely common among infantry, airborne, and other physically demanding military occupations. Despite being very common, many veterans don't realize these conditions are ratable — or that chronic residuals from repeated stress fractures can support ongoing disability compensation. Here's exactly how the VA rates them.

🦵 Shin Splints Fast Facts

The VA rates shin splints (medial tibial stress syndrome) and stress fractures primarily under Diagnostic Code 5022 (periostitis) or 5263 (genu recurvatum) depending on residuals. Ratings range from 10–30% based on chronic symptoms. Secondary knee and back claims are common.

How the VA Rates Shin Splints and Stress Fractures

Shin splints are rated under DC 5022 (periostitis — inflammation of the bone lining) when chronic. Stress fractures with residual effects are also rated under musculoskeletal codes based on their location and functional impact:

ConditionDiagnostic CodeRating RangeWhat It Requires
Chronic shin splints (periostitis)DC 502210–30%Chronic pain, tenderness on exertion, documented history
Tibia/fibula stress fracture residualsDC 526210–30%Residual deformity, limitation of motion, or chronic pain
Impairment with limited motionDC 5270–527410–40%Range of motion limitations in ankle from stress fracture

Proving Chronic Shin Splints for Service Connection

Many veterans had shin splints documented during service but received a rating of 0% because they appeared to "resolve." The key is showing chronic residuals — ongoing pain, tenderness, or functional limitation even after the acute episode resolved. Document:

  • In-service medical records showing repeated shin splint or stress fracture diagnoses
  • Current symptoms — pain with exercise, tenderness along the tibia, night pain, swelling
  • Any permanent changes — periosteal thickening, callus formation, or bone remodeling visible on imaging

Secondary Conditions

  • Ankle instability — Stress fractures and shin splints alter gait and stress the ankle. See our ankle guide.
  • Knee conditions — Altered running mechanics from shin pain stresses knees. See our knee guide.
  • Back conditions — Compensatory movement patterns cause lumbar strain. See our back pain guide.
  • Flat feet — Often a contributing cause and co-occurring condition. See our flat feet guide.
