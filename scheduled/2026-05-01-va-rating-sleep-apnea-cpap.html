VA Rating for Sleep Apnea with CPAP: Why It's Always 50% (2026)

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

If you have obstructive sleep apnea and your doctor has prescribed a CPAP, BiPAP, or APAP machine, the VA is required to rate your condition at 50%. Many veterans with CPAP prescriptions are incorrectly rated lower. Here's exactly why the 50% rating applies and what to do if yours is wrong.

⚡ The Rule Is Simple

VA regulations under 38 CFR Part 4, Diagnostic Code 6847 state: Sleep apnea that requires use of a breathing assistance device = 50% rating. If you have a CPAP prescription and are rated at anything less than 50%, your rating is likely incorrect and should be appealed. A 50% rating is worth $1,075.16/month tax-free.

The 50% Rule — What "Requires" Actually Means

The rating criteria says "requires use of a breathing assistance device" — not "uses a breathing assistance device." This distinction matters. If your doctor prescribed the CPAP, you require it medically. Whether you actually use it consistently does not matter for the rating. The prescription itself establishes the requirement.

What Counts as a Breathing Assistance Device

  • CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure)
  • BiPAP (Bilevel Positive Airway Pressure)
  • APAP (Auto-adjusting Positive Airway Pressure)
  • Adaptive servo-ventilation (ASV)

Oral appliances (mouth guards) and positional therapy devices generally do NOT qualify as breathing assistance devices for the 50% rating — only machines that provide positive airway pressure count.

If You Were Rated Below 50% with a CPAP

  1. Gather your CPAP prescription documentation from your doctor
  2. File a Supplemental Claim (VA Form 20-0995) with the CPAP prescription as new evidence
  3. Alternatively, request a Higher-Level Review if the error is clear from existing records
  4. The VA should correct the rating to 50% upon review

See our appeal guide for the full process on challenging incorrect ratings.

The Proposed VA Rule Changes — What to Know

The VA has proposed changes to sleep apnea ratings that could change how CPAP-treated asymptomatic sleep apnea is rated. As of 2026, the current 50% CPAP rule remains in effect. However, veterans are strongly advised to file sleep apnea claims under current rules immediately rather than waiting — ratings established before any rule changes take effect are "grandfathered" in.
