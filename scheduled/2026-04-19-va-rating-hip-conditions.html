VA Rating for Hip Conditions: Complete 2026 Guide

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Hip conditions are very common among veterans — particularly those who served in physically demanding roles. Hip bursitis, arthritis, labral tears, and the need for total hip replacement are all ratable conditions. Here's exactly how the VA rates hip conditions in 2026.

🦴 Hip Condition Fast Facts

The VA rates hip conditions primarily under DC 5250–5256 based on range of motion and functional limitation. Hip replacement is rated at 100% for one year after surgery, then drops to 30% minimum. Secondary back and knee claims are very common with hip conditions. Use our combined rating calculator.

Total Hip Replacement — Special Rules

Veterans who have undergone total hip replacement are rated at 100% for one year following surgery. After the one-year period, the VA evaluates residual disability — the minimum rating after hip replacement is 30%, regardless of how well the surgery went. Document any continued pain, range of motion limitations, or functional problems after surgery to support the highest post-surgical rating.

Hip Bursitis

Greater trochanteric bursitis — inflammation of the bursa on the outer hip — is rated under DC 5252 as limitation of extension. Many veterans with back injuries develop hip bursitis as a secondary condition due to altered gait mechanics.

Secondary Conditions

  • Lumbar spine (back) — Hip and back conditions are biomechanically linked. See our back pain guide.
  • Knee conditions — Altered gait from hip problems stresses the knees. See our knee guide.
  • Flat feet — Often part of the same biomechanical chain. See our flat feet guide.
  • Depression from chronic pain — Rates separately. See our mental health guide.
