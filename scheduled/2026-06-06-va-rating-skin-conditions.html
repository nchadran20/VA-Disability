VA Rating for Skin Conditions: Eczema, Psoriasis, Dermatitis (2026)

Updated: June 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Skin conditions — eczema, psoriasis, contact dermatitis, and other chronic skin disorders — are among the top 20 most claimed VA conditions. Veterans exposed to chemical agents, extreme weather, and environmental hazards develop chronic skin conditions at higher rates than civilians. Here's how the VA rates them in 2026.

🩺 Skin Condition Fast Facts

The VA rates most skin conditions under Diagnostic Code 7806 (dermatitis/eczema) or condition-specific codes. Ratings are based on percentage of body surface affected and whether systemic therapy (pills, injections) versus topical treatment (creams) is required. Higher body surface involvement + systemic therapy = higher rating. Use our combined rating calculator.

How the VA Rates Skin Conditions (DC 7806 — Dermatitis/Eczema)

RatingCriteriaMonthly Pay (Alone, 2026)
0%Topical therapy required less than 6 weeks per year AND less than 5% of total body surface affected$0 (establishes service connection)
10%Topical therapy required for 6 weeks or more per year AND at least 5% but less than 20% of total body surface affected (or if intermittently systemic therapy required)$175.51
30%Systemic therapy (pills, injections) required for 6 weeks or more per year AND at least 5% but less than 20% of total body surface affected$524.31
60%Systemic therapy required for 6 weeks or more AND 20–40% of total body surface affected$1,361.88

Key Rating Factors to Document

  • Percentage of body surface affected — Be precise with your doctor. The 5%, 20%, and 40% thresholds are pivotal for rating levels. Ask your dermatologist to document the exact body surface area affected in your records.
  • Type of therapy required — Systemic therapy (Methotrexate, biologics, oral steroids, immunosuppressants) results in a dramatically higher rating than topical treatment alone. Make sure your records clearly distinguish between topical and systemic treatments.
  • Weeks per year of treatment — The 6-week threshold matters. Document how many weeks per year active treatment is required.

Psoriasis — Specific Considerations

Psoriasis is rated under DC 7816. The criteria mirror DC 7806 — body surface area and systemic therapy are the key factors. Psoriatic arthritis (joint inflammation from psoriasis) is rated separately under musculoskeletal codes and can add significantly to a combined rating. If you have psoriasis with joint involvement, claim the arthritis separately.

Service Connection for Skin Conditions

Common service connection pathways:

  • Direct — Exposure to chemicals, fuels, solvents, herbicides (Agent Orange), or extreme environmental conditions during service
  • Secondary to PTSD — Stress is a well-established trigger for psoriasis and eczema flares. Secondary service connection through PTSD is medically supported.
  • Secondary to medications — Some service-connected conditions require medications that cause or worsen skin conditions
