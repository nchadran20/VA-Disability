VA Higher-Level Review: What It Is and When to Use It (2026)

Updated: May 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

The Higher-Level Review (HLR) is one of three appeal options available when the VA denies your claim or rates you too low. It's the fastest of the three options and best used in specific situations. Here's exactly when to use it and how to file.

📋 HLR Fast Facts

Processing time: 4–5 months average. What it is: A senior VA rater reviews your existing evidence for clear errors. What it is NOT: A new evidence submission (use Supplemental Claim for that). Success rate: Approximately 30–40% of HLRs result in a favorable change. File deadline: Within 1 year of your rating decision.

When to Use a Higher-Level Review

HLR is best used when:

  • The VA made a clear factual or legal error in applying the rating criteria
  • The C&P examiner's opinion was inadequate or contained errors
  • The VA failed to consider all of your submitted evidence
  • Your condition clearly meets the criteria for a higher rating but was rated lower
  • You do NOT have new evidence to submit (if you do, use a Supplemental Claim instead)

When NOT to Use an HLR

  • You have new evidence the VA hasn't seen — use a Supplemental Claim instead
  • You want a formal hearing or oral argument — file directly with the Board of Veterans Appeals
  • Your claim was denied because you lack service connection evidence — HLR can't create evidence that doesn't exist

How to File a Higher-Level Review

  1. Complete VA Form 20-0996 — Decision Review Request: Higher-Level Review
  2. Clearly identify which issues you are appealing (specific conditions and rating decisions)
  3. Optionally request an informal conference with the senior rater — this is a brief phone call where you can point out the specific error you believe was made
  4. File within 1 year of your rating decision to preserve your effective date
  5. Submit the form online at va.gov, by mail, or through your VSO

The Informal Conference — Use This Option

When filing an HLR, you can request an informal conference — a brief phone call with the senior rater assigned to your claim. Use this call to specifically point out the error in the original decision. "The examiner rated my knee at 10% but my range of motion measured 30° of flexion which under DC 5260 should produce a 20% rating" is the type of specific, clear point that works well in an informal conference.
