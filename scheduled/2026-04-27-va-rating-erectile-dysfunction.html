VA Rating for Erectile Dysfunction: What Veterans Need to Know (2026)

Updated: April 2026 Read time: 9 min 2026 Rates Applied

Erectile dysfunction is one of the most commonly missed VA disability claims. Nearly every veteran with a service-connected condition that could cause ED qualifies for an additional $126.77/month through Special Monthly Compensation — and the vast majority never file the claim. Here's exactly how it works.

💰 ED Compensation Fast Facts

VA compensation for erectile dysfunction is paid through SMC-K (Special Monthly Compensation) — an additional $126.77/month on top of your existing disability pay, tax-free. It requires no new disability rating — just service connection and a current diagnosis. Almost any service-connected condition can support this claim.

How the VA Compensates for Erectile Dysfunction

ED itself is rated at 0% under Diagnostic Code 7522. However, that 0% rating combined with service connection qualifies you for SMC-K — Special Monthly Compensation for "loss of use of a creative organ." The 0% rating establishes service connection, and the SMC-K adds $126.77/month automatically. See our complete SMC guide for full details on all SMC levels.

Service Connection Pathways for ED

You can service connect ED directly or as secondary to any of these common service-connected conditions:

Primary ConditionConnection to ED
PTSDPTSD medications (SSRIs, SNRIs) commonly cause ED as a side effect; PTSD itself disrupts sexual function
DiabetesDiabetic neuropathy damages the nerves controlling erectile function
HypertensionBoth hypertension and its medications (beta blockers, diuretics) cause ED
Lumbar spine injuryNerve damage from back injuries affects erectile function
Prostate conditionsProstate surgery and radiation directly affect erectile function
Peripheral vascular diseaseReduced blood flow affects erectile function

How to File the Claim

  1. Get a current diagnosis of ED from your doctor — a simple note in your medical records stating the diagnosis is sufficient
  2. Get a statement from your doctor (nexus letter) connecting the ED to your service-connected condition — "at least as likely as not that [service-connected condition] caused or contributed to the veteran's erectile dysfunction"
  3. File VA Form 21-526EZ listing ED as a new condition secondary to your existing service-connected condition
  4. Request SMC-K evaluation in your claim

See our nexus letter guide for exactly how to get proper documentation from your doctor.
